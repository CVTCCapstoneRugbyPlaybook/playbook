Laws

Rugby Basics:

Rugby union is played by two teams of 15 Players.
The field of play in rugby is refered to as 'The Pitch'
You can run with the ball, kick it and pass it. Unlike American football forward passing is not allowed; instead passes are lateral or backwards.
A game of rugby union has two periods of 40 minutes each. In international matches the referee will stop the clock for stoppages.
Between the halves, there is a 10-minute interval, after which both teams change ends.
The start of each half is indicated by the referee's whistle.
Before the start of the match, the referee tosses a coin to decide which team will kick off the match.
The game is started by a place kick or a drop kick from the middle of the halfway line.
The ball must travel forwards at least 10 metres from the kick-off.

Objective:

The objective of rugby is to Score more points than your opponent!
