About Rugby


What is Rugby?

Often called the "game played in heaven." Rugby is a full-contact sport that evolved from football (i.e. soccer). Rugby is a free-flowing game which requires a combination strength, speed and strategy to move a ball into an opponents territory.


Where was Rugby developed?

Rugby was developed at Rugby School in Rugby, Warwickshire, England. one of many versions of football played at English public schools in the 19th century.Rugby was developed in the 19th Century.


Rugby's ancient history.

The Ancient Greeks and Romans are known to have played a ball game that involved the use of their feet. There is a Roman game called harpastum which is believed to have been adapted from a Greek team game known as Episkyros or phaininda. The games appear to have resembled rugby football.


The rest is history.

In 1871, English clubs met to form the Rugby Football Union (RFU). In 1892, the existing rugby union authorities responded to charges of professionalism made against some clubs for paying players for missing work. Sanctions were issued against the clubs, players, and officials involved in the new organization, commonly refered to as the Northern Union (NU) or Northern Rugby Football Union. Separate clubs were formed and named "rugby league" and "rugby union".


Rugby ball.

An official rugby union ball is oval and made of four panels. It has a length in-line of 280–300 millimetres, a circumference, end to end, of 740–770 millimetres, and a circumference, in width, of 580–620 millimetres. The ball is made of leather or suitable synthetic material, and may be treated to make it water resistant and easier to grip. The weight of a rugby ball may not be more than 460 grams or less than 410 and has an air pressure of 65.71–68.75 kilopascals, or 0.67–0.70 kilograms per square centimetre, or 9.5–10.0 lbs per square inch.


Rugby uniform.

Rugby shirts were formerly made of cotton but are now made of a cotton and polyester mix. This material has the advantage of not absorbing as much water or mud as cotton alone. The rugby jerseys are slightly different depending on the type of rugby game played. The shirts worn by rugby league players commonly have a large "V" around the neck. The players in rugby union wear jerseys with a more traditional design. The number of the player and his or her surname are placed on the upper back of the jersey, and the logo of the team on the upper left chest.
